Facts

The word duke comes from latin dux (leader)

the first duke was chosen in 1337

the first marquess was chosen in 1385

The feudal system in medieval Europe was a hierarchical structure with the king at the top, followed by barons, lords, and peasants.

Peasants made up around 90% of the population and had a hard life, often working long days and barely having enough food to survive.

The king believed in the "divine right" to rule, and the Catholic Church was very powerful, with bishops managing areas called dioceses and receiving a tithe of 10% from all the people.

The lords and barons ruled large areas of land called fiefs and were responsible for maintaining an army at the king's service.

The local lord ran the manor, which was the center of life in the Middle Ages, and peasants worked the land in exchange for protection.

Land was granted to people in exchange for service, creating a system of loyalty and obligation.

When a king died, his firstborn son would inherit the throne, and a long-lasting family rule was called a dynasty.
